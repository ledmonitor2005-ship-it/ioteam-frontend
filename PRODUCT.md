# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Factory owners, energy managers, and sustainability/ESG officers at Vietnamese industrial enterprises (manufacturing plants, food processing, textiles, smart agriculture). They are visiting during a procurement or evaluation cycle, under pressure to cut operating costs and meet ISO 50001 or ESG reporting requirements. They are skeptical of vendor promises and respond to real data, real ROI numbers, and evidence of local deployments.

## Product Purpose

An EnMS (Energy Management System) platform that gives industrial enterprises real-time visibility into energy consumption across all assets and facilities. The product solves the inability to identify where energy is wasted, enables ISO 50001 compliance, and connects IoT sensors to a unified dashboard. Success looks like a factory reducing energy costs measurably within the first quarter and passing an energy audit with confidence.

## Brand Personality

Modern, Innovative, Eco-forward. The site should feel like a technically credible partner that also cares about sustainability — not a startup pitching, but an expert showing their work. Vietnamese industrial pragmatism with green-tech ambition.

## Anti-references

- Generic SaaS / startup aesthetic: blue-gradient heroes, floating UI orbs, abstract blob illustrations, Intercom / Notion / Linear lookalike layouts.
- Heavy consultancy sites with dense text, gray palettes, or PDF-style layouts.
- Greenwashing aesthetics: leaf icons, stock-photo trees, superficial eco-branding that feels performative.

## Design Principles

1. **Credibility through data** — Lead with specific numbers (kWh saved, % cost reduction, real project ROI). Vague claims are invisible to an engineer.
2. **Show, don't tell** — The dashboard screenshot earns more trust than a feature list. Let the product be visible.
3. **Green is earned, not decorated** — The emerald palette reflects the mission. Avoid gratuitous leaf icons or eco-theater; the color is enough.
4. **Vietnamese-first clarity** — Copy, context, and social proof fit the local industrial enterprise context. Not a translated Western SaaS.
5. **Industrial confidence** — Tone and visual weight should feel like a confident engineering firm, not a fundraising startup.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA minimum. Vietnamese text requires careful font rendering — Be Vietnam Pro is the primary choice. Ensure reduced-motion alternatives for scroll animations. Color contrast must hold for body text on the mint-green background (#f0faf4).
